## Campaign Ideas

### Campaign 1: "I Already Told You That" Video Series

**Category**: Acquisition + Awareness
**Channel**: Social Media (LinkedIn, Twitter, TikTok)

#### Concept
Create a comedic video series dramatizing frustrating moments when people have to repeat informationâ€”customer service calls, meetings, onboarding new team members. Each video ends with: "What if you never had to repeat yourself again? Introducing Dossier."

#### Why It Could Work
- Relatable pain point that resonates emotionally
- Shareable, meme-able content format
- Works across professional and consumer contexts
- Low production cost if done authentically
- High virality potential with right execution

#### Execution Outline
1. Script 5-7 scenarios (15-30 second videos each)
2. Film with simple setup or user-generated submissions
3. Launch 1 video every 2 days across platforms
4. Create hashtag #INeverRepeatMyself challenge
5. Encourage users to share their "repeat yourself" horror stories
6. Retarget video viewers with feature demo ads

#### Resources Needed
- Video production (in-house or freelancer): $2-5K
- Social media ad budget: $3-5K for amplification
- Community manager time for engagement
- Landing page for feature with demos

#### Success Metrics
- Video views (target: 500K combined)
- Share rate (target: >5%)
- Landing page visits (target: 50K)
- Feature trial sign-ups (target: 2,500)
- Cost per acquisition

#### Risks & Mitigations
- **Risk**: Humor doesn't land or seems tone-deaf
  - **Mitigation**: Test with focus group, A/B test different scenarios
- **Risk**: Feature benefit unclear from creative
  - **Mitigation**: Clear end card with specific value prop and demo link

### Campaign 2: "The Repetition Tax Calculator"

**Category**: Acquisition (Product-Led Growth)
**Channel**: Content Marketing + SEO

#### Concept
Build an interactive calculator that shows users how much time and money they waste repeating themselves. Input: role, team size, hours in meetings. Output: "You waste 127 hours per year repeating information. That's $8,340 in lost productivity. Dossier saves you X hours."

#### Why It Could Work
- Interactive tools drive 2x engagement vs static content
- Creates concrete, personalized value proposition
- Highly shareable (people love sharing personalized results)
- Strong SEO value for "productivity tool" searches
- Captures qualified leads who see their pain quantified

#### Execution Outline
1. Research time-wasting statistics and build calculation methodology
2. Design simple, mobile-friendly calculator interface
3. Create dedicated landing page with SEO optimization
4. Add social share buttons with pre-populated "I waste X hours repeating myself" messages
5. Gate detailed report/PDF behind email capture
6. Set up nurture sequence for calculator users
7. Outreach to productivity bloggers/newsletters for coverage

#### Resources Needed
- Developer time: 20-30 hours
- Designer: 10 hours
- Research/copywriting: 15 hours
- Email sequence setup: 5 hours
- PR outreach list and time

#### Success Metrics
- Calculator uses (target: 25K in first month)
- Email captures (target: 30% conversion)
- Social shares (target: 5K)
- Organic traffic growth
- SQLs from calculator leads (target: 500)

#### Risks & Mitigations
- **Risk**: Calculator feels gimmicky or numbers seem inflated
  - **Mitigation**: Base on real research, cite sources, be conservative with estimates
- **Risk**: Low traffic to tool
  - **Mitigation**: Paid promotion, influencer partnerships, product hunt launch

### Campaign 3: "Memory Lane" Customer Story Campaign

**Category**: Retention + Advocacy
**Channel**: Email + Social Proof Hub

#### Concept
Profile 10-15 power users showing "before Dossier" chaos vs "after Dossier" efficiency. Create mini-documentaries (60-90 seconds) + written case studies. Focus on the emotional relief of never repeating yourself. Build dedicated hub page as social proof library.

#### Why It Could Work
- Social proof is most powerful conversion driver
- Real stories > marketing claims
- Gives existing users recognition (increases loyalty)
- Creates reusable content for sales team
- Demonstrates diverse use cases

#### Execution Outline
1. Identify 10-15 diverse user profiles (roles, industries, use cases)
2. Interview customers via Zoom (record for video testimonials)
3. Create 3 formats: 60-sec video, written case study, quote cards
4. Build "Customer Stories" hub on website
5. Email campaign to prospects: "See how [Role] uses Dossier"
6. Sales enablement package with case studies
7. Paid social promotion of video stories
8. Monthly spotlight in newsletter

#### Resources Needed
- Customer success time for outreach: 10 hours
- Video editor: 20 hours
- Writer for case studies: 30 hours
- Web development for hub: 15 hours
- Customer incentives: $200-500 per participant

#### Success Metrics
- Customer participation rate (target: 50%)
- Case study views (target: 15K)
- Time on page (target: 2+ minutes)
- Conversion rate of case study visitors (target: 8%)
- Sales team usage rate

#### Risks & Mitigations
- **Risk**: Customers decline or testimonials feel staged
  - **Mitigation**: Offer meaningful incentives, use professional interviewer, keep it conversational
- **Risk**: Stories don't differentiate or feel repetitive
  - **Mitigation**: Curate diverse use cases, focus on specific outcomes

### Campaign 4: "30-Day Repetition Detox Challenge"

**Category**: Activation
**Channel**: Email + In-App + Community

#### Concept
Create a guided 30-day challenge for new Dossier users to build the habit of documenting information once. Daily prompts, tips, and milestones. Gamification with badges. Community element where participants share progress. Goal: Turn trial users into committed daily users.

#### Why It Could Work
- Addresses core SaaS challenge: getting users to habit formation
- Creates engagement loop during critical first 30 days
- Community element increases retention
- Gamification drives completion
- Can be evergreen after initial launch

#### Execution Outline
1. Design 30-day curriculum (what to add to Dossier each day)
2. Create email sequence + in-app notifications
3. Build badge/progress system in product
4. Set up Slack/Discord channel or in-app community
5. Create support content (videos, tips, templates)
6. Launch cohort 1 with promotional push
7. Weekly live Q&A sessions with product team
8. Celebrate completers with swag/recognition
9. Analyze retention data and optimize

#### Resources Needed
- Product manager: 40 hours (curriculum design)
- Developer: 60 hours (gamification features)
- Designer: 20 hours
- Email/copy: 15 hours
- Community manager: 10 hours/week during challenge
- Swag budget: $50 per completer

#### Success Metrics
- Challenge enrollment rate (target: 40% of new users)
- Completion rate (target: 45%)
- Day 30 retention vs non-participants (target: +35%)
- Daily active usage increase
- NPS score of participants

#### Risks & Mitigations
- **Risk**: Challenge feels like work rather than value-add
  - **Mitigation**: Keep daily time investment under 5 minutes, emphasize benefits
- **Risk**: Low completion rate
  - **Mitigation**: Reminders, community accountability, meaningful rewards

### Campaign 5: "The Anti-Meeting Meeting" Thought Leadership Push

**Category**: Acquisition (Brand Building)
**Channel**: Content + PR + Speaking

#### Concept
Position Dossier as part of broader movement against inefficient communication. Publish manifesto "The Anti-Meeting Playbook" with specific tactics for async communication. Launch with PR push, speaking tour, podcast appearances. Core message: The best meeting is the one you don't have because information is already captured.

#### Why It Could Work
- Taps into widespread meeting fatigue post-pandemic
- Creates bigger narrative than just a feature
- Drives thought leadership and brand authority
- Media loves contrarian takes on work culture
- Attracts partnership opportunities

#### Execution Outline
1. Write comprehensive "Anti-Meeting Playbook" (25-40 pages)
2. Design beautiful PDF + dedicated microsite
3. PR outreach to business/tech media (TechCrunch, Forbes, Fast Company)
4. Secure 5-7 podcast appearances for CEO/founder
5. Submit to speak at HR/productivity conferences
6. LinkedIn thought leadership content series
7. Partner with async-first companies (GitLab, Doist) for co-promotion
8. Create "Anti-Meeting Certified" badge for companies who adopt principles
9. Host webinar series with remote work influencers

#### Resources Needed
- Writer/editor: 60 hours for playbook
- Designer: 30 hours
- PR agency or in-house: $5-10K/month for 3 months
- Web development: 20 hours
- Speaking/travel budget: $5K
- Partnership development time

#### Success Metrics
- Media placements (target: 8-10 tier 1/2)
- Playbook downloads (target: 50K)
- Speaking engagements booked (target: 5)
- Brand search volume increase (target: +200%)
- Partnership deals signed (target: 3)
- Inbound leads (target: 1,500)

#### Risks & Mitigations
- **Risk**: Message seems anti-collaboration or too extreme
  - **Mitigation**: Frame as pro-efficiency, not anti-meeting; include nuance
- **Risk**: Low media pickup
  - **Mitigation**: Tie to data/research, time with return-to-office news cycle
